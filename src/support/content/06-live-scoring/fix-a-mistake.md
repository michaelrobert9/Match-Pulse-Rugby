# Fix a mistake during a match

Scoring live means the odd slip: a try to the wrong team, the wrong scorer, or a penalty logged that shouldn't have been. While the match is live, these are quick to put right.

## Fix a scoring event

1. Find the event in the match's timeline.
2. Reverse it if it shouldn't be there — the score drops back by that event's points (a try by 5, a penalty by 3, and so on).
3. Re-add it against the correct team or type if needed, and credit the right player.

## Common fixes

- **Wrong scorer, right team:** credit the event to the correct player from the timeline. The team score stays the same; the player credit moves.
- **Wrong team:** reverse the event and re-add it for the correct team.
- **Try that shouldn't count** (held up, called back): reverse it. The score drops back by its points — and the conversion, if one was recorded, must be reversed separately.
- **Try that should have been a penalty try:** open the event and change its type — the score adjusts by the difference.

## Do it while you're live

The best time to fix a scoring mistake is during the match, before you finalise. Everything's still open and a correction is just a tap.

Once the match is finalised, the result is locked and feeds the log and stats. Fixing it then is a result correction, which is more involved. See [Correct a finalised result](correct-a-finalised-result.md).

## Before you finalise

When the match ends, take a moment to check the score, the try count and the scorers look right before locking it in. A quick review at full time saves a correction later. See [Finalise a result](finalise-a-result.md).
