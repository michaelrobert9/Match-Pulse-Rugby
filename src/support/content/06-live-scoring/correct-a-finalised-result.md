# Correct a finalised result

Sometimes a mistake only comes to light after a match is finalised: a wrong score, a misattributed goal, a result that was confirmed too early. You can still fix it. Because everything is calculated from the source result, correcting the fixture and re-running the numbers puts the records straight.

## How it works

MatchPulse builds standings and stats from the actual results, not by adding and subtracting as it goes. So when you correct a finalised fixture:

1. You change the result on the fixture itself.
2. The standings recompute to reflect the corrected result.
3. The stats are rebuilt from the source, so player and competition records update to match.

You're not patching the table by hand. You fix the source, and the rest follows.

## Steps

1. Open the competition.
2. Go to the **Fixtures** section.
3. Open the finalised fixture.
4. Make the correction: fix the score, the scorer, or the outcome.
5. Confirm the correction.

The affected standings and stats update from the corrected result.

## Things to know

- **Fix the source, not the symptom.** If the standings look wrong, the cause is almost always a wrong result. Correct the fixture, and the table sorts itself out. See [Why a stat looks wrong](../07-stats-standings/why-a-stat-looks-wrong.md).
- **Knockout brackets follow too.** If a corrected result changes who won, the team that advances updates with it. See [How teams advance](../05-playoffs/how-teams-advance.md).
- **Career stats rebuild.** Player career records recompute from match results, so a correction flows through to a player's career totals as well.

## Better still

Corrections work, but checking carefully before you finalise is always cleaner. See [Finalise a result](finalise-a-result.md).
