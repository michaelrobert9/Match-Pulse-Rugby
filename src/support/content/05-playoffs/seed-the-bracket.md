# Seed teams into the bracket

Seeding is how you place teams in a knockout bracket so the matchups are fair and the strongest teams don't meet too early. A well-seeded bracket keeps the best contests for the later rounds.

## Why seed at all

Without seeding, the draw is essentially random, and the two best teams could end up facing each other in the first round. Seeding ranks the teams and places them so the top seeds are kept apart until deeper in the bracket.

## How seeds are decided

It depends on the competition:

- **Hybrid competition:** the league or group phase usually decides the seeds. The team that topped the table is the top seed, and so on down. The standings give you the order. See [How standings are calculated](../07-stats-standings/how-standings-work.md).
- **Standalone knockout:** you set the seeds yourself, based on whatever ranking makes sense, past results, an external ranking, or organiser's choice.

## Steps

1. In the playoff builder, after setting the bracket shape, place each team into a seed position.
2. Put the top seed and the lowest seed on opposite ends so they can only meet in the final.
3. Fill the rest of the bracket so higher seeds face lower seeds in the early rounds.
4. Save.

## Standard knockout seeding

A typical eight-team bracket pairs them 1 v 8, 4 v 5, 3 v 6, 2 v 7, which keeps the top two seeds apart until the final. MatchPulse helps you lay this out when you build the stage.

Once you're happy with the seeding, the first-round fixtures are set. To change the layout before matches are played, see [Edit the bracket](edit-the-bracket.md).
