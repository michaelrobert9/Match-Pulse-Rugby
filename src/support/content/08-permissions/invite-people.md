# Invite people and assign roles

As an Owner, you can bring other people in to help run your competition, and decide exactly what each can do.

## Before you start

Decide two things:

1. **What role** they need: Owner-level to manage with you, or Scorer to score matches. See [Roles explained](roles-explained.md).
2. **What scope**: the whole competition, or just one team. See [Team-scoped vs competition-scoped roles](scoped-roles.md).

## Steps

1. Open the competition (or team) you want to give access to.
2. Go to the **People** or **Roles** section.
3. Choose **Add** or **Invite**.
4. Enter the person's details (usually their email).
5. Pick the **role**.
6. Set the **scope**, the whole competition or a specific team.
7. Confirm.

The person now has the access you granted and will see the relevant competition or team on their dashboard.

## A worked example

You run a school league with eight teams. You want each coach to score their own team's matches, and a colleague to help you manage the whole thing.

- Give your colleague a competition-scoped Owner-level role.
- Give each coach a Scorer role scoped to their own team.

Now everyone can do their part, and only their part.

## Good practice

- **Give the least access that does the job.** A coach who only scores doesn't need Owner rights.
- **Use team scope for coaches**, so they can't touch other teams' results.
- **Keep Owner-level access to people you fully trust** to manage the competition.

## Removing access

When someone no longer needs access, take it away. See [Remove someone's access](remove-access.md).
