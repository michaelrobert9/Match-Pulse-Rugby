# Team-scoped vs competition-scoped roles

A role can apply to a whole competition or to just one team within it. Choosing the right scope means people get exactly the access they need and nothing more.

## Competition-scoped

A role at competition level applies across the whole competition. A competition-scoped Scorer can score any match in the competition, whichever teams are playing. Good for an organiser or a dedicated scorer covering everything.

## Team-scoped

A team-scoped role applies to just one team. A team-scoped Scorer can score that team's matches, but not anyone else's. This is the natural fit for a coach or manager who's responsible for their own side and shouldn't be touching other teams' results.

## Why this matters

In a league with many teams, you don't want every coach able to score every match. Team-scoping lets you give each coach access to their own team's fixtures only. Everyone can help, but they stay in their lane, and there's no risk of one team's manager accidentally changing another's result.

## How it works

When you grant someone a role, you choose its scope: the whole competition, or a specific team. The same Owner or Scorer role becomes broad or narrow depending on the scope you give it.

## Choosing the scope

- **One person covering the whole event?** Competition scope.
- **A coach handling only their team?** Team scope, set to that team.

To grant a role and set its scope, see [Invite people and assign roles](invite-people.md).
