# Roles explained: Owner and Scorer

MatchPulse uses roles to control who can do what. This lets you bring people in to help, a coach here, a scorer there, without handing over control of everything.

## Owner

The Owner has full authority. Whoever creates an organisation or a competition is its Owner. An Owner can:

- Edit settings, teams and fixtures.
- Score and finalise matches.
- Resolve non-standard outcomes.
- Grant roles to other people.

The Owner is the final say. Everyone else operates within the limits the Owner sets.

## Scorer

A Scorer can score matches: start a match live, record goals and events, and finalise the result. A Scorer can't reshape the competition, change its settings, or grant roles to others.

This is the role for someone whose job is to score on the day, a team manager, a coach, a parent helping out, without giving them control over the wider setup.

## Why roles are separated this way

Running a competition usually involves several people. The organiser sets it all up. Coaches or volunteers score their matches. Keeping Owner and Scorer separate means each person can do their part without being able to accidentally change things outside their job.

## Scoping a role

Roles can be limited to a single team rather than the whole competition. A team's coach can be a Scorer for just that team's matches, and nothing else. This is one of the most useful things about the system. See [Team-scoped vs competition-scoped roles](scoped-roles.md).

## Granting roles

Owners hand out roles. See [Invite people and assign roles](invite-people.md). For how authority flows from the Owner down, see [Competition ownership and authority](../03-competitions/ownership-and-authority.md).
