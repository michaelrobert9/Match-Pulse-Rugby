# Org types: Schools, Clubs, Associations, Franchises

When you create an organisation, you pick one of four types. The type matters because it affects how your organisation fits into the wider structure and how it connects to others.

## The four types

**School**
A school running its own teams and fixtures. Often part of a wider association, which you can link as a parent.

**Club**
A sports club, usually with several teams across age groups or divisions. Like a school, a club can sit under an association.

**Association**
A governing or organising body that runs competitions across multiple schools or clubs. Associations are often the parent organisation for the schools and clubs that play in their leagues.

**Franchise**
A franchise structure, typically used where teams operate under a shared brand or umbrella across regions.

## How types connect

The four types aren't isolated. A school or club can belong to an association by setting that association as its **parent organisation**. This is what lets a wider body run a competition that pulls in teams from many member organisations.

For example, an association might run the league. Each school is its own organisation with the association set as its parent. The school manages its own teams; the association manages the competition they all play in.

## Choosing the right type

Pick the type that describes what the organisation actually is, not what it does. A school is a School even if it's the one organising a friendly tournament. The competition format is set separately, on the competition itself.

To set up the parent link, see [Link a parent organisation](link-a-parent-organisation.md).
