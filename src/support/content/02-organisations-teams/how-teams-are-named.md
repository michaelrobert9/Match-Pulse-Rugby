# How teams are created and named

Teams live inside an organisation. A team is what gets entered into competitions and what plays in fixtures. Getting the naming right from the start saves confusion later, especially once a team appears across standings, fixtures and stats.

## Where a team's name comes from

A team belongs to an organisation, and its name usually reflects both the organisation and what makes that specific team distinct. The organisation gives it identity; the team name distinguishes it from the organisation's other teams.

For a school called Riverside College, you might have:

- Riverside College 1st XI
- Riverside College 2nd XI
- Riverside College U16A

The school is the organisation. Each XI or age-group side is a separate team within it.

## Naming conventions

A few habits keep things tidy:

- **Be consistent.** Pick one format and stick to it across all your teams. Mixing "1st XI" and "First Team" makes lists harder to read.
- **Include the level or age group** where it matters, so "U16A" and "U16B" are easy to tell apart.
- **Keep it recognisable.** The name shows up in fixtures and standings, often next to other organisations' teams. It should be clear who the team is at a glance.
- **Avoid clutter.** You don't need to repeat the season or competition in the team name. That context comes from the competition the team is entered into.

## How naming plays out in competitions

When a team is entered into a competition, its name carries through to the fixture list and the standings table. That's why consistency matters: a clean, recognisable name reads well everywhere it appears, and avoids the awkward case of two teams that look almost identical.
