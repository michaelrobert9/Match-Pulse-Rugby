# Competition naming conventions

A competition's name is how people find and recognise it. A clear name reads well in lists, on standings tables and anywhere the competition is shared. A vague one causes confusion, especially when you run several competitions a season or across years.

## What a good name includes

Aim for a name that answers three things at a glance: who it's for, what it is, and when.

- **Who or what level:** the age group, division or grade. "U16", "1st Team", "Premier Division".
- **What kind of competition:** "League", "Cup", "Tournament", "Knockout".
- **When:** the season or year, so this season's competition is easy to tell apart from last season's.

Put together, you get names like:

- Coastal Schools U16 League 2026
- North Coast 1st XI Cup 2026
- Riverside Invitational Tournament 2026

## Habits that keep it clean

- **Be consistent across competitions.** If you run several, use the same naming pattern for all of them. It makes your whole list easy to scan.
- **Include the year or season.** This is the single most useful thing for telling competitions apart over time. Without it, "U16 League" could be any year.
- **Don't overload the name.** You don't need to cram in the organisation, the venue and the format all at once. Keep it readable.

## Why it matters more than it looks

The competition name appears wherever the competition does: on the fixture list, the standings, and any link you share. A consistent, dated name means anyone, including people outside your organisation, knows exactly what they're looking at. It also keeps your own admin sane once you've run a few seasons.
