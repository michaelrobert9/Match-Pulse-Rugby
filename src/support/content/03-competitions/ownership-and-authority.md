# Competition ownership and authority

Every competition has a clear line of authority. This decides who can change what, and it stops two people accidentally undoing each other's work.

## The Owner

Whoever creates a competition is its Owner. The Owner has full authority over it:

- Edit the competition's settings and name.
- Add and remove teams.
- Add, edit and delete fixtures.
- Score and finalise matches.
- Grant other people roles on the competition.

## The authority chain

Authority flows down from the Owner. The Owner can give other people roles, such as a Scorer for a particular team or the whole competition. Those people can do what their role allows, but they can't override the Owner or grant authority they don't have themselves.

This chain keeps control clear. The Owner is always the final say on their competition. People they bring in help run it within the limits they've been given.

## Roles you can grant

- **Owner**-level authority for someone you fully trust to manage the competition with you.
- **Scorer** for someone who only needs to score matches, either for one team or across the competition.

Roles can be scoped to a single team, so a team's coach can score that team's matches without touching anyone else's. See [Team-scoped vs competition-scoped roles](../08-permissions/scoped-roles.md).

## Why it works this way

A competition often has many people involved: an organiser, a few coaches, someone scoring on the day. The authority chain means everyone can do their part without stepping on each other, and the person who owns the competition stays in control of it.
