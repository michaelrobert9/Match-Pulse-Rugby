# Choose a format: league, knockout, hybrid

When you create a competition, you pick how it's structured. This shapes how fixtures work and how teams progress.

## League

Every team plays a set of fixtures, and standings are built from the results. Teams earn points for wins and draws, and the table ranks them. Good for a season where everyone plays everyone, or a round-robin group.

The standings table is the heart of a league. It recalculates from the results as fixtures are finalised, so it's always up to date.

## Knockout

Teams are placed into a bracket and play elimination rounds. Win and you advance; lose and you're out. Good for cups and tournaments where you want a single winner at the end.

Knockout structures are built with the playoff builder. See [Build a knockout stage](../05-playoffs/build-a-knockout-stage.md).

## Hybrid

A mix of both. A common shape is a league or group phase that feeds into a knockout. Teams play a round-robin first, the standings decide who qualifies, and the top teams go into a bracket to decide the winner.

This is how a lot of real tournaments run: pool play followed by playoffs. You set up the league phase, and when it's done, build the knockout stage from the qualifying teams.

## Choosing

- Want a table and everyone playing through a season? **League.**
- Want a straight tournament with a single winner? **Knockout.**
- Want group play that leads into finals? **Hybrid.**

You set the format when you [create the competition](create-a-competition.md). For the knockout side of things, the [Playoffs section](../05-playoffs/build-a-knockout-stage.md) covers building and seeding the bracket.
